Understanding Your Options

Caveat Loan vs Mortgage Loan — What's the Difference?

Tembusu offers two property-backed financing products. Both are TDSR-exempt and secured against your property — but they serve different situations. Here's how to tell them apart and choose the right one for your business.

🔖

Caveat Loan

Second-charge financing on an already-mortgaged property

  • Best for Businesses with an existing mortgage needing additional working capital without refinancing
  • Security Second-charge caveat lodged on the property title — sits behind existing mortgage
  • LTV Up to 80% of property value (combined with existing mortgage)
  • Processing Faster — lower legal complexity than a full mortgage
  • Property types Residential, commercial, industrial — including properties with existing bank loans
🏦

Mortgage Loan

First-charge financing on an unencumbered or refinanced property

  • Best for Businesses with an unencumbered property seeking maximum loan quantum
  • Security First-charge mortgage registered on the property title — priority security position
  • LTV Up to 80% of property value as a standalone first mortgage
  • Processing Slightly longer — full mortgage registration required
  • Property types Residential, commercial, industrial — property must be unencumbered or fully refinanced

Choose a Caveat Loan if…

Your Property Already Has a Mortgage

  • You need working capital but don't want to disturb your existing mortgage
  • You need funds quickly — caveat registration is faster than a full mortgage
  • You want to top up available equity without refinancing your current loan
  • Your business needs short-to-medium-term capital for growth or cash flow
Choose a Mortgage Loan if…

Your Property is Unencumbered

  • Your property is fully paid up and you want maximum loan quantum
  • You are refinancing an existing mortgage and topping up simultaneously
  • You need a larger loan amount than caveat equity allows
  • You prefer a first-charge security structure for a longer-term facility

How Businesses Used Second-Charge Equity to Move Fast

A caveat loan works precisely when most lenders won't — when there's already a mortgage on the property and the business still needs capital. Here's how two of our clients put it to work.

Trading — Inventory Financing

Trading Company Secures Peak Season Inventory After Bank Declines Top-Up

🏢 Shophouse · Chinatown 📅 Funds in 4 days
$450K Loan Amount
68% Combined LTV
4 days To Disbursement
The Situation

A consumer goods importer needed $450K to place orders ahead of the Chinese New Year season — their highest revenue period of the year. The company's director owned a Chinatown shophouse with an existing bank mortgage. The bank declined a top-up loan, citing their internal LTV ceiling already being reached under MAS guidelines. Approaching a second bank would take weeks; the supplier's order window closed in five days.

What Tembusu Structured

Tembusu assessed the shophouse at current market value. With the existing bank mortgage, the combined LTV came to 68% — within Tembusu's parameters. A caveat was lodged on the title as a second charge, and the $450K facility was approved within 24 hours. Legal was completed within three days, and funds were disbursed on day four.

The Outcome
Orders placed on time Peak season fulfilled Loan repaid in 9 months
"We'd never heard of a caveat loan before. Within a week we had the funds and the orders were in. The bank took three weeks just to tell us no." — Director, Consumer Goods Importer, Singapore
Property Investment — Acquisition Deposit

Investor Uses Condo Equity to Fund Commercial Unit Deposit Without Refinancing

🏠 Private Residential · Novena 📅 Funds in 5 days
$320K Loan Amount
71% Combined LTV
5 days To Disbursement
The Situation

A private investor had identified a strata retail unit in a suburban mall — priced attractively and selling below market due to the vendor's urgency. The 20% option deposit of $320K was due within a week. The investor owned a Novena condominium with a long-term bank mortgage and had substantial equity, but refinancing would take 4–6 weeks and incur exit penalties on the existing loan. He needed a way to access the equity without touching the mortgage.

What Tembusu Structured

Tembusu lodged a second-charge caveat against the Novena condo, preserving the existing bank mortgage entirely. The combined LTV across both the bank loan and Tembusu's caveat was 71%. The $320K was approved within one business day and disbursed on day five following caveat registration. The existing mortgage was undisturbed; no exit penalties were incurred.

The Outcome
Option exercised on time Existing mortgage intact Commercial unit acquired
